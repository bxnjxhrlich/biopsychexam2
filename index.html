⚡ ACTION POTENTIALS

Resting potential = −70 mV. K⁺ most permeable at rest (leak channels).

Membrane = phospholipid bilayer + ion channel proteins. Selectively permeable.

Ions at rest: Outside = Na⁺, Cl⁻. Inside = K⁺, P⁻ (proteins).

Forces on ions: electrostatic + concentration gradients.

Equilibrium potentials: K⁺ ≈ −85mV, Cl⁻ ≈ −66mV, Na⁺ ≈ +55mV. Ca²⁺ similar pressures to Na⁺.

Ion channel gating: leak (always open), voltage-gated (APs), chemical/ligand-gated, mechanical, temperature, acidity.

AP Steps

1. Threshold (−55mV) → 2. Na⁺ channels open, Na⁺ rushes IN → +40mV → 3. Na⁺ channels inactivate → 4. K⁺ channels open (delayed), K⁺ rushes OUT → repolarization → 5. K⁺ channels slow to close → hyperpolarization (−90mV) → 6. Return to −70mV

All-or-none: AP fires fully or not at all. Intensity encoded by firing rate.

Refractory Periods

Absolute: CANNOT fire. Na⁺ channels inactivated.

Relative: CAN fire but needs stronger stimulus. Membrane hyperpolarized (K⁺).

Na⁺/K⁺ Pump

3 Na⁺ OUT, 2 K⁺ IN. Uses ATP. Against gradients. Maintains resting potential.

Propagation

AP goes one direction only — behind it = refractory period (Na⁺ inactivated).

Fastest: large + myelinated (saltatory conduction — jumps node to node).

Slowest: small + unmyelinated.

EPSPs, IPSPs, Summation

EPSP = small depolarization (closer to threshold). IPSP = small hyperpolarization (farther).

Dendrites/soma = graded responses (not all-or-none).

Summation: neuron adds all EPSPs & IPSPs. Threshold reached → AP fires.

🔗 SYNAPTIC COMMUNICATION

Otto Loewi (1921)

Stimulated vagus nerve of frog heart 1 → slowed. Heart 2 in same bath also slowed. Proved chemical communication. Vagusstoff = acetylcholine (ACh). Parasympathetic branch. 1936 Nobel Prize.

Synapse & Transmission

Synapse = gap where NT is released & binds receptors.

AP arrives → Ca²⁺ channels open → Ca²⁺ triggers exocytosis → vesicles fuse → NT spills into cleft → binds receptors → EPSP/IPSP → NT deactivated

Exocytosis requires CALCIUM (Ca²⁺)!

NT Deactivation

Reuptake (pumped back in) · Enzymatic degradation (e.g., AChE breaks down ACh) · Diffusion (drifts away)

Electrical vs Chemical Synapses

Electrical (gap junctions) = FASTER, ions pass directly, bidirectional.

Chemical = slower, NT-mediated, more flexible, usually unidirectional.

Receptor Types

Ionotropic = FAST. NT binds → ion channel opens directly. Ex: nicotinic ACh receptor.

Metabotropic = SLOW but long-lasting. NT binds → G-protein → second messengers → can alter gene expression. Ex: NE receptors.

GABA (inhibitory) → opens Cl⁻ channels at GABA-A → hyperpolarization → IPSP.

🔬 METHODS

Histology & Tracers

Histology = study of microscopic tissue structure. Fix brain → slice thin → stain → view under microscope.

Golgi stain: few neurons, FULL shape (Cajal used it → Neuron Doctrine).

Nissl stain: ALL cell bodies, no dendrites/axons.

Retrograde tracers: go BACKWARD to cell body. "Where do inputs come from?"

Anterograde tracers: go FORWARD to terminals. "Where do outputs go?"

Immunohistochemistry: labeled antibodies find specific proteins/NTs in tissue.

EEG diagnostics: epilepsy, sleep disorders, brain death. Can pick up muscle artifact (EMG).

Evoked potential: EEG response time-locked to a stimulus (average many trials).

Invasive Techniques

Stereotaxic device: holds head fixed, uses 3D coordinates to target brain regions precisely.

Lesions → what function is LOST. Methods: aspiration, electrolytic, excitotoxic (most precise — spares passing axons).

Stimulation → what function is PRODUCED. Complements lesion studies.

Unit recording: microelectrodes record neurons. Single-unit (1 neuron) → multi-unit (small group) → field potentials (large population).

Genetic Tools

Inbred: genetically identical → eliminates genetic variability.

Knockout: gene deleted → see what goes wrong → reveals gene function.

Transgenic: foreign gene inserted → study its effects (e.g., human disease gene).

⚠️ No viral vectors or optogenetics on exam.

Animal Ethics

FOR: life-saving treatments, animals benefit too, tightly regulated.

AGAINST: suffering concerns, species differences, improving alternatives.

IACUC: reviews/approves all protocols. Must include 1 vet + 1 unaffiliated community member.

Animal Welfare Act: housing/feeding/handling/vet care standards; must show research is necessary & alternatives not feasible; minimize pain/distress; annual USDA reporting; IACUC mandated.

💊 PHARMACOLOGY

Basics

Psychoactive drug: affects the nervous system to alter perception/mood/behavior.

Drugs act at synapses. Must cross BBB to reach brain. Once in blood → distributed in seconds to minutes.

Routes (fast → slow)

IV (fastest) → Inhalation → IM → SC → Oral (slowest).

Faster routes = quick peak but DON'T maintain blood levels as long. Slower = gradual, sustained.

BBB & Lipid Solubility

BBB = tight junctions + lipid membrane. Fat-soluble crosses; water-soluble can't.

Heroin > morphine because heroin is more lipid-soluble → crosses BBB faster.

Non-drowsy antihistamines: less lipid-soluble → can't cross BBB → no brain sedation.

Yes, some psychoactive drugs do NOT cross the BBB (affect PNS only).

Drug Metabolism

Drugs broken down by enzymes in the liver.

Acetaldehyde = toxic metabolite from alcohol breakdown. Normally converted to harmless acetic acid.

Antabuse blocks the enzyme that degrades acetaldehyde → accumulates → nausea/vomiting → deters drinking.

Agonists & Antagonists

Agonist = mimics/enhances NT effect. Antagonist = blocks NT effect.

Drug Mechanisms ⭐

↑↓ synthesis · block vesicle storage · ↑↓ release · activate receptors (agonist) · block receptors (antagonist) · block reuptake (SSRIs, cocaine) · block enzymatic degradation (MAO inhibitors)

Dose-Response & Tolerance

Dose-response curve: dose (x) vs effect (y). Different effects of same drug = different curves.

Full agonist asymptote = max receptor activation (all occupied).

Tolerance = diminished response after repeated use. Shifts curve RIGHT (need more drug).
