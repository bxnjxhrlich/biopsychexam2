1 · Drugs

Tolerance

↓responsiveness, need more for same effect.

  • Metabolic: body breaks drug down faster (liver↑)
  • Functional: target less responsive — Cellular (receptor↓) or Conditioned (Siegel)
  • Cross-tol: tol to 1 drug → tol to another (same system)
  • Sensitization: opposite. Bigger response. Dose-curve shifts LEFT

Withdrawal

Adverse effects of stopping. WD = drug effects FLIPPED.

Opiate fx
drowsy, constipation, ↓resp, ↓HR, ↓pain, ↓BP, euphoria
Opiate WD
insomnia, diarrhea, ↑resp, ↑HR, ↑pain, ↑BP, depression

Alcohol WD — 4 stages

  1. Tremors, ↑HR, HTN, sweat, insomnia (hrs)
  2. Hallucinations (1–2 days)
  3. Delirium, delusions, disorient, amnesia
  4. Seizures

Alcohol WD more dangerous than opiate WD.

Why over-excited? EtOH = GABA+/Glu−. Brain compensates: ↓GABA, ↑Glu. Remove EtOH → unopposed → too little inhib + too much excit → seizures.

Conditioned tolerance (Siegel)

Environment = CS → triggers compensatory CR opposite to drug fx.

  • Adams '69: max tol in drug-assoc env
  • Siegel '77: same dose in new env → overdose
  • Falls '89: more WD in drug-assoc env

2 · Reward · ICSS

Olds & Milner (1954)

Rats self-stimulate brain via lever (ICSS) — thousands/hr, cross electrified floor, skip food/water/sleep. These sites normally mediate pleasure of natural rewards.

ICSS sites

  • Hypothalamus
  • Septum
  • Nucleus accumbens (NAc)
  • Ventral tegmental area (VTA)
  • Medial forebrain bundle — most effective

DA ↔ ICSS ↔ Drug abuse

All converge on mesolimbic/mesolimbocortical DA (VTA→NAc).

  • ICSS ↑ DA release in NAc
  • DA agonists ↑ ICSS; DA antagonists block it
  • Lesions of mesolimbocort pathway → ↓ICSS
  • Drugs of abuse hijack same circuit

Microdialysis

Measures NT release in vivo.

  1. Probe stereotaxically implanted
  2. Fluid samples collected
  3. Analyzed by HPLC (separates by MW)

Conditioned Place Preference

Drug in env A, saline in env B. Later choose freely. Prefers drug env → drug is rewarding. DA agonists → CPP.

4 · Emotion

Darwin

Emotional expression is innate + evolved. Evidence:

  • Similar in lower animals
  • Present in very young children
  • Same in congenitally blind

Evolved from behaviors that signal what animal does next. Opposite msgs → opposite behaviors.

James-Lange

Stim → body reaction → interpreted as feeling.
"bear → HR↑ → therefore afraid"

Counter-evidence:

  • Epi injection → no emotional feelings alone
  • No peripheral sensation → still feel emotion

Cannon-Bard

Stim → body reaction AND feeling simultaneously, independently.
"bear → HR↑ AND afraid (parallel)"

JL = sequential. CB = parallel.

5 · Fear · Amygdala

LeDoux: Low / High road

┌─ HIGH → CORTEX ─┐
stim → THAL AMYG → fear
└─── LOW ───────────┘
  • LOW: thal→amyg. Fast, crude. "Flinch from stick-that-might-be-snake"
  • HIGH: thal→cortex→amyg. Slow, accurate. Confirms/cancels

Observing fear in rodents

  • Freezing
  • ↑ HR, ↑ startle
  • ↓ salivation
  • Defecation
  • Hypoalgesia (↓pain reactivity)
  • ↓ activity, ↓ appetite

Main paradigm: Pavlovian fear conditioning (tone+shock).

Conditioned fear → Amygdala

Lesions in rats/rabbits/mice disrupt it. Human SM (Urbach-Wiethe): amyg damage → conditioned fear deficit. fMRI: amyg activates during fear cond. (Skip specific pathways.)

BLA vs CE

CS ─┐
├→ BLA → CE → behaviors
US ─┘ (learn) (output)
  • BLA (basolateral complex) = INPUT. Fear learning. Pairs CS+US. = lateral + basolateral amyg
  • CE (central nucleus) = OUTPUT. Activated by BLA. Coordinates fear response (freeze, startle, sympathetic)

"BLA learns, CE acts."

6 · Learned Helplessness

Manipulation

Controllability. Both groups get identical shock; only one can stop it.

Paradigm

Yoked tailshock. Control group: terminates shock via wheel. Uncontrol group: same shock, no wheel. Only uncontrol → helpless.

Effects (BLUE list)

Almost all ↓, except ↑ fear/anxiety/ulcers/drug reward.

  • ↓ shock responsive, ↓ struggle in water
  • ↓ aggression, ↓ social dominance
  • ↓ defensive behaviors
  • ↓ eating, drinking, sex
  • Analgesia (↓pain sens)
  • Learning impairments
  • Inappropriate maternal behavior
  • ↓ social interaction
  • ↑ anxiety, ↑ fear-learning
  • ↑ ulcers
  • Some drugs of abuse more rewarding

Depression model

Good: profile mirrors human depression (motivation, appetite, sex, anxiety, cognition).

Flaw + Maier: models uncontrollability specifically — one aspect relevant to depression AND anxiety/PTSD. Not depression per se.

Uncontrollability does: ↓ fight/flight (escape), ↑ fear & anxiety.

Autoreceptor

Presynaptic, inhibitory receptor regulating its own NT release. Neg feedback / homeostatic. DRN 5-HT1A autoreceptors are postsynaptic but same function.

DRN — necessary & sufficient

Uncontrol shock activates DRN 5-HT neurons much more than control (Grahn '99). Activate DRN → helpless. Block DRN during uncontrol → no helpless.

DRN hypothesis

UNCONTROL STRESS
↓
↓ 5-HT1A autoreceptor function
↓
DRN sensitized (more 5-HT)
├→ AMYGDALA → ↑ fear
└→ dPAG → ↓ escape

mPFC

Normally inhibits DRN. Executive/emotion regulation. Detects controllability → blocks helplessness. Shutting off mPFC → LH even with control! mPFC = "I've got this" signal.

7 · Anxiety · BNST · Tx

Anxiety disorders

Hypothesis: amygdala overactive — responds too strongly, or to things that shouldn't elicit fear. PTSD fMRI: exaggerated amyg to masked faces (Rauch/Whalen). (Skip Predatory Imminence.)

BNST vs Amygdala

AMYG
Fear-potentiated startle — discrete, brief CS + shock. Phasic.
BNST
Light-enhanced startle — sustained ambient threat. Also: CRF-startle, predator odor, long-CS fear, context fear, LH, stress-reinstate drug seek, conditioned defeat

Fear vs Anxiety

  • Fear (AMYG): discrete, predictable, short, phasic. "bear in front of me"
  • Anxiety (BNST): diffuse, unpredictable, sustained, uncertain timing. "bear might appear"

BNST organizes responses to stimuli that poorly predict when danger occurs. Human fMRI: BNST ↑ in anticipatory anxiety, approaching tarantula, hypervigilance, sustained unpredictable threat.

Allosteric modulator · Valium

Allosteric mod: binds a different site than natural NT. Changes receptor shape so NT becomes more (+) or less (−) effective. Doesn't activate alone.

Valium (benzo): positive allosteric mod @ GABAA. Makes GABA better at opening Cl− channel → ↑ inhibition → ↓ anxiety. Needs GABA to be there. Alcohol also facilitates GABA.

Serotonin in anxiety

Confusing field. Depends on: (1) receptor subtype (>18), (2) chronic vs acute, (3) brain region. 5-HT1A KO mice → ↑ anxiety.
